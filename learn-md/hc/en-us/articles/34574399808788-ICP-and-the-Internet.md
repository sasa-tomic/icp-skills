# ICP and the Internet

The Internet Computer aims to extend the functionality of the internet beyond simply connecting devices and networks, but running applications in a tamper-proof manner. The first articles in this section, describe how users' requests and the Internet Computer's responses are transferred and verified

- [Edge infrastructure:](/hc/en-us/articles/34212818609684) how request and responses reach their destination, including caching and load-balancing
- [HTTP Gateway Protocol:](/hc/en-us/articles/34211943471892) how HTTPs is translated into the Internet Computer Protocol
- [Asset certification:](/hc/en-us/articles/34276431179412) how responses from the Internet Computer are verified

The second part of this section explains how canisters can communicate with web2 servers and use their data for computations without trusted intermediaries.

- [HTTPS outcalls:](/hc/en-us/articles/34211194553492)  how canisters can interact with web2
